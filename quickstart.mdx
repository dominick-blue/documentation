## Get Started in Five Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Your First Project">
    Projects are containers for your research. Each project holds its own papers, notes, drafts, and code snippets.

    1. Click **Projects** in the sidebar
    2. Click **New Project**
    3. Enter a name (e.g., "ML Healthcare Survey")
    4. Select a template: **Paper**, **Grant**, **Thesis**, or **Default**
    5. Click **Create**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Import a Paper">
    Add your first PDF to the library.

    1. Navigate to **Library** in the sidebar
    2. Click **Import Paper** or drag a PDF onto the page
    3. Review the extracted metadata (title, authors, year)
    4. Click **Import**

    <Tip>Your paper now appears in the library grid.</Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read and Annotate">
    Open a paper and create your first annotation.

    1. Click on a paper card to open the PDF viewer
    2. Select text in the PDF
    3. A highlight popover appears:
       - Choose a **category** (Key Claim, Method, Critique, etc.)
       - Add a **note** (optional)
       - Click **Save**
    4. View all annotations in the right sidebar
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a Note">
    Start connecting your ideas.

    1. Navigate to **Notes** in the sidebar
    2. Click **New**
    3. Enter a title and start writing in Markdown
    4. Use `[[Note Title]]` to link to other notes
    5. Use `@paper-title` to reference papers
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start a Draft">
    Begin writing your manuscript.

    1. Navigate to **Drafts** in the sidebar
    2. Click **New**
    3. Enter a title for your manuscript
    4. Write in Markdown with live preview
    5. Click the **Cite** button to insert citations from your library
    6. Click **Export** when ready to output to PDF, DOCX, or LaTeX
  </Step>
</Steps>
